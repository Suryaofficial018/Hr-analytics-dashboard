## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes **15,000+ employee records** to identify key drivers of attrition, performance trends, salary distribution, and workforce insights using **Microsoft Excel** and **Power BI**.

**Tools Used:** Microsoft Excel (Data Cleaning, Pivot Tables) · Power BI (DAX, Dashboard Development)

---

## ❗ Problem Statement

HR departments often face challenges such as:
- High employee attrition with no clear root cause
- Low employee satisfaction going undetected
- Difficulty identifying workforce and performance trends
- Inefficient employee performance tracking
- Salary optimization challenges

Without proper data analysis, organizations struggle with employee retention, workforce planning, and productivity improvement. This project solves these challenges using HR analytics and interactive dashboards.

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives

- Clean and preprocess raw HR data in Excel
- Analyze employee demographics and attrition patterns
- Study department-wise performance and salary trends
- Identify job satisfaction and work-life balance trends
- Build a 3-page interactive Power BI dashboard for HR decision-making

## 💡 Business Recommendations

1. **Training for Entry-Level Employees** — Age group 18–30 has 51% attrition. Companies should provide structured onboarding, mentorship, and team management support for new joiners.

2. **Salary Increments & Promotions** — Education role employees leave due to low pay (49% attrition). Annual salary reviews and performance-based promotions should be implemented across all roles.

3. **Recognize High Performers** — High performers need recognition and rewards. Low performers need guidance and motivation rather than pressure, to improve retention.

4. **Improve Work-Life Balance** — Employees with poor work-life balance have 60% attrition. Flexible scheduling and reduced after-hours pressure can significantly improve retention.

5. **Remote Work for Long-Distance Employees** — Employees commuting 60–100 km have 52% attrition. Weekly remote work options or flexible timings can reduce this significantly.
